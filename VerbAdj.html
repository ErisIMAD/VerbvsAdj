What are verbs?

Here's how Verbs are different from Adjectives

  • Verbs are action words that express what a subject does or the state of being it is in.
  • Verbs can convey a wide range of actions, from physical movements (e.g., "run" or "jump")
    to mental processes (e.g., "think" or "believe").
  • Verbs are dynamic elements of language that not only signify actions and states but also
    contribute to the structure of sentences by establishing subject-verb agreement, tense,
    aspect, mood, and voice. They are essential for conveying the nuances of communication,
    such as indicating whether an action is ongoing, completed, or hypothetical.
